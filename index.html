The most frequent urban manifestations of barricades during summit protests are burning dumpsters or cars. Since many major European cities have organized their trash system underground, however, dumpsters are vanishing from the imagery of street confrontations. Like Haussmann reacting to the frequent barricading of Paris's streets in the 19th century by planning broader avenues (Douglas 2008), contemporary city planners diminish the likelihood of disruption of urban flows by emptying the streets of objects useful for such a purpose.
